## Candidate Directions

### KapitBiz Relay / closed rescue dispatch

Ranks at-risk inventory and reserves pre-approved freezer, generator, dry-storage, courier, or temporary selling capacity. Main challenges: covariant disaster risk, marketplace cold start, custody, food safety, host incentives, and a build-heavy demo.

### Resilience Receipt / event-triggered loss evidence

Creates a pre-event inventory baseline, records event and downtime evidence, captures protective actions and actual damage, and assembles an SB Corp ERF- or insurance-oriented evidence packet. Main challenges: generic OCR/PDF framing, owner recordkeeping burden, fraud, and unvalidated institutional acceptance.

### DiskarteWatt / outage and spoilage economics

Estimates outage cost, inventory spoilage risk, protective actions, and backup-power economics for an individual business. Main challenges: calculator-like demo, assumptions about loss, and outage demand potentially declining after the Davao Light transition.

### Stronger alternative

Either model may propose another concept, but it must show stronger Tagum evidence, an honest one-day implementation, and a complete three-to-five-minute demo loop.

## Required Evaluation

Evaluate every serious recommendation on:

1. Evidence of Tagum MSME demand, not merely evidence that the problem exists.
2. Novelty relative to checklists, information portals, calculators, document generators, and common hackathon insurance projects.
3. Feasibility for three developers in one day using the existing Next.js/localStorage foundation.
4. Whether the demo reveals or hides the hardest real-world dependency.
5. Covariant disaster risk and whether the solution still works during a city-scale event.
6. Dependence on unavailable utility feeds, insurers, lenders, LGUs, suppliers, landlords, or marketplace participants.
7. Measurable continuity or recovery impact without invented metrics.
8. Buyer, user incentive, adoption burden, and plausible pilot path.
9. The strongest judge objection and a truthful response.
10. Explicit kill criteria that would make the team abandon the concept.

## Output Standard

- Label claims as verified fact, inference, or unsupported assumption.
- State where previous research confuses problem evidence with product demand.
- Penalize seeded demos that manufacture unavailable partners or live data.
- Do not reward polite agreement.
- Concede points when the opposing evidence is stronger.
- Recommend one MVP, one fallback, and concepts to kill.
- End with confidence from 0-100% and the next validation action that could change the verdict.
