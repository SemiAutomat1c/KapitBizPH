# Judge Q&A One-Pager — print this, hold it during Q&A

**Rule:** answer the question asked → give a local user + a number → then STOP talking.
The person handling Q&A holds this. Don't over-explain; a tight answer beats a long one.

---

## The 9 questions judges almost always ask

**1. Who is your first user?**
> One named person or segment in Tagum. e.g. "A sari-sari owner in Magugpo East" / "A banana aggregator near Madaum" / "A backyard tilapia operator in Bincungan." Never "everyone."

**2. Why would they use this instead of their current manual way?**
> It's faster, cheaper, or it catches what they miss today. Name the manual pain: "Right now they check Facebook for scrap prices / have no plan when a storm hits / find out about a pH crash after the fish die."

**3. Who pays, and how much?**
> Name ONE payer + a rough figure. Don't say "everyone pays a little." e.g. "The MSME pays a small commission once it saves them money," or "The LGU/Negosyo Center sponsors onboarding," or "Circulab pilot funding up to ₱1.3M."

**4. What can you pilot in Tagum in 30 days?**
> Small and concrete: one barangay cluster / one market / one co-op, onboarded via the Negosyo Center. Week 1 onboard, Weeks 2–3 run the loop, Week 4 measure one result.

**5. What data do you need and how will you get it?**
> Minimal. The user provides it at intake (the form/photo/reading). For baseline numbers, one partner introduction. Don't claim you need a big dataset.

**6. How does this create income, jobs, savings, or resilience?**
> Tie to the impact metric. "More predictable earnings for local producers" (Wadhwani/Chamber language), not just "digitalization."

**7. How do you prevent fake reports or low-quality listings?**
> Photo + geotag + timestamp on entries; partner/co-op verification for the pilot; status confirmed by the receiving side (e.g. operator confirms the load).

**8. What works offline / under weak internet?**
> The core loop runs on-device and syncs when back online. (True — the app defaults to a local store; this is a real strength at a Tagum venue.)

**9. Doesn't this already exist?** ← the one that trips teams up
> "Yes — TrashCash, PDRF's checklists, and commercial pond sensors exist. They serve Manila consumers, large firms, or ₱50k budgets. **None reach a sari-sari store or a 500 m² fishpond in Tagum. Our innovation is distribution:** Taglish, offline-capable, free at point of use, deployed through the Negosyo Center in a 30-day pilot." Competitors = proof of demand.

## Do / Don't

- DO name a real barangay, a real number, a real partner.
- DO admit what you haven't built yet, then say the 30-day plan for it.
- DON'T say "everyone," "the whole Philippines," or "save the environment."
- DON'T oversell AI — say what it does for the user, not that it's AI.
