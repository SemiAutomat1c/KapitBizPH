## Strategic Read

This is not just a generic student hackathon. The partner mix suggests judges will reward ideas that:

- help real MSMEs, farmers, fisherfolk, or local processors;
- show a circular economy loop, not only "AI for X";
- can be piloted in Tagum or Davao del Norte after the event;
- produce income, jobs, cost savings, waste diversion, or continuity after disaster;
- have a clean demo that feels like a small finished product.

The safest pitch language:

- "for Tagum MSMEs"
- "waste-to-value"
- "business resilience"
- "pilot-ready"
- "income pathway"
- "local value chain"
- "blue economy" if aquaculture/fisheries is assigned
- "circular economy" and the 10 Rs if the discussion is sustainability-heavy

## Partner Cheat Sheet

### DTI Davao del Norte / Negosyo Center Tagum City

Likely lens: MSME usefulness, business registration/readiness, market access, advisory support, and digitalization.

What to emphasize:

- This is simple enough for a sari-sari store, farmer, processor, vendor, or fishpond operator to use.
- It can be introduced through Negosyo Center assistance, trainings, or MSME Week follow-through.
- It creates a clear next step after the hackathon: onboarding local MSMEs.

Pitch line:

> "This is not only an app. It is a Negosyo Center-ready tool for helping local MSMEs turn waste, risk, or operational problems into income and continuity."

### Tagum City Business and Incentive Center

Likely lens: local economic development, investment promotion, business climate, and city-level implementation.

What to emphasize:

- Make Tagum the center of the story, not just "Mindanao" or "the Philippines."
- Show how the city could pilot it with one barangay, market, cooperative, or MSME cluster.
- Tie it to business friendliness and investment readiness.

Pitch line:

> "Tagum can pilot this as a city-level circular economy and MSME resilience layer."

### Tagum City Chamber of Commerce and Industry, Inc.

Likely lens: private-sector practicality and whether members would actually use it.

Useful source facts:

- The Chamber says it exists to promote and uphold Tagum's economic interests.
- Its mission includes unifying local MSMEs, equipping entrepreneurs, and building practical linkages.
- It grew from 15 founding members to over 120 active members.

What to emphasize:

- Clear business model and user pain.
- No heavy behavior change or complex setup.
- A demo that shows a business owner saving time, money, or lost sales.

Pitch line:

> "A Chamber member should understand the value in under 30 seconds: more buyers, less waste, faster recovery, or lower operating risk."

Source: https://tagumcitychamber.com/web/about-us.html

## Judge Q&A Prep

Expect these questions:

- Who exactly is your first user?
- Why would they use this instead of their current manual process?
- Who pays, and how much?
- What can you pilot in Tagum within 30 days?
- What data do you need and how will you get it?
- How does this create income, jobs, savings, or resilience?
- How do you prevent fake reports or low-quality marketplace listings?
- What part works offline or under weak internet?
- What makes this circular economy, not just a generic app?

Best answer pattern:

1. Local user.
2. Pain today.
3. One working demo loop.
4. Measurable impact.
5. Pilot path with partner.

Example:

> "Our first user is a banana smallholder or aggregator near Tagum. Today, pseudostems and rejects are discarded or underpriced. Our demo lets them list usable waste by photo and location, then matches it to processors or makers. In a 30-day pilot, we only need one barangay cluster, one processor, and one Negosyo Center onboarding session."
